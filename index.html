“Everyone told us we’d be back in six months. They were wrong.”

The decision didn’t happen overnight. It built slowly — over years of open-plan offices and conversations about “someday.” In 2017 we started researching boats seriously. In 2018 we stopped researching and started packing.

We chose a Leopard 40 catamaran — built new at Robertson & Caine in South Africa — and named her Oroboro, after the ancient symbol of a serpent eating its own tail. Renewal. We took delivery in Cape Town in September 2018, with almost no offshore experience between us.

The first passage was a shakedown sail to Langebaan, three hours up the South African coast. Then north to Namibia, across a desert coastline that felt like the moon. Then the South Atlantic — 2,800 miles of open ocean, just the two of us, learning what we were actually capable of.

The Route

Trinidad to Grenada, then Covid closed the world. An unplanned eighteen months became some of the finest sailing we’ve done.

We’re Francesco and Yuka. We met in San Francisco, worked in technology, and spent years telling ourselves we’d do something like this eventually. In 2018, we stopped saying eventually.

Oroboro is our home — a Leopard 40 catamaran built in 2018. She carries us, all our gear, a surprising amount of wine, and the ongoing project of figuring out what we actually want from a life. She’s named for the ouroboros, the ancient symbol of renewal. It still feels accurate.

We document the journey because we wished someone had been more honest with us before we left. The good parts are extraordinary. The hard parts are real. The whole thing is worth it.

This site is the log we were looking for in 2017. If you’re out here thinking about doing something like this, we hope it helps.
